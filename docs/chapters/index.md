# Chapters

This textbook is organized into 16 chapters covering 275 concepts across the Theory of Knowledge curriculum.

## Chapter Overview

1. [Foundations of Knowledge](01-foundations-of-knowledge/index.md) - Core epistemological building blocks: knowledge, belief, truth, perspective, justification, and bias.
2. [Theories of Truth and Knowledge](02-theories-of-truth-and-knowledge/index.md) - Competing theories of truth, justified true belief, the Gettier Problem, and types of knowledge.
3. [Evidence and Justification](03-evidence-and-justification/index.md) - Types of evidence, methods of inquiry, source evaluation, and standards of credibility.
4. [Knowledge and the Knower](04-knowledge-and-the-knower/index.md) - How identity, culture, emotion, and perception shape what we know.
5. [Cognitive Biases](05-cognitive-biases/index.md) - Systematic errors in reasoning from confirmation bias to cognitive dissonance.
6. [Reasoning and Argumentation](06-reasoning-and-argumentation/index.md) - Argument structure, inductive and deductive reasoning, and logical fallacies.
7. [Knowledge and Language](07-knowledge-and-language/index.md) - How language enables and constrains knowledge: ambiguity, translation, rhetoric, and metaphor.
8. [Skepticism, Intellectual Virtues, and Knowledge Production](08-skepticism-and-intellectual-virtues/index.md) - Skeptical traditions, intellectual courage and honesty, and how knowledge is produced and validated.
9. [Areas of Knowledge and Mathematical Methods](09-areas-of-knowledge-and-mathematics/index.md) - The eight IB Areas of Knowledge, plus axioms, proof, formal systems, and Gödel.
10. [Natural Sciences and the Scientific Method](10-natural-sciences-and-scientific-method/index.md) - Scientific method, falsifiability, paradigms, Kuhn, pseudoscience, and the demarcation problem.
11. [Human Sciences and History](11-human-sciences-and-history/index.md) - Qualitative and quantitative methods, historical inquiry, revisionism, and oral traditions.
12. [The Arts as Knowledge](12-the-arts-as-knowledge/index.md) - Aesthetic knowledge, artistic interpretation, creativity, and art's relationship to emotion and culture.
13. [Ethics and Values in Knowledge](13-ethics-and-values-in-knowledge/index.md) - Moral reasoning frameworks, ethical dilemmas, research ethics, and hermeneutics.
14. [Knowledge, Technology, and Power](14-knowledge-technology-and-power/index.md) - AI, algorithms, big data, epistemic injustice, gatekeeping, and marginalized knowledge.
15. [Misinformation and the Information Age](15-misinformation-and-the-information-age/index.md) - Misinformation, fact-checking, media literacy, deepfakes, echo chambers, and conspiracy theories.
16. [TOK Assessment and Synthesis](16-tok-assessment-and-synthesis/index.md) - Knowledge questions, the TOK essay and exhibition, and synthesizing epistemological insights.

## How to Use This Textbook

This textbook follows a carefully designed sequence where each chapter builds on concepts from earlier chapters. The dependency structure ensures you always have the vocabulary and context needed before encountering new ideas. While you can explore chapters of particular interest, working through them in order will provide the most coherent learning experience.

**Note:** Each chapter includes a list of concepts covered. Make sure to complete prerequisites before moving to advanced chapters.
